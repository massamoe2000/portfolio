Rise An Acrobatic Play

Mubarak is the executive director of “Rise, An Acrobatic Play,” a unique, high-energy, awe-inspiring production that combines theatre, acrobatics and dance for an entirely new theatrical experience. Written and directed by four-time NCAA Gymnastics Champion Taqiy Abdullah-Simmons, choreographed by former U.S. Tumbling and Trampoline National Team Member Jeffrey Brown, produced by GymCrew Freestyle Acrobatics Co-Founders Kerry Adderly, combined with an all-star cast, Rise tells the story of high school students who hope to become world-renowned entertainers. Rise is a moving, futuristic coming-of-age story about a group of high school students dreaming of being world-class entertainers.

My Profile3

Mubarak Abdullah-Simmons

Strategy Consultant

Mubarak Abdullah-Simmons from Philadelphia, also known as Mo, is a GymCrew strategy consultant and a stuntman for Walt Disney Worlds Indiana Jones Epic Stunt Spectacular Show.
Mubarak attended the University of Oklahoma on a full athletic scholarship and capped off his competitive gymnastics career by winning the 2005 NCAA Team Championships with his younger brother Taqiy Abdullah, and was subsequently honored at the White House on Champions Day by President George W. Bush.
Mubarak received his B.S. in Political Science from the University of Oklahoma in 2006. It was there that he developed his passion for understanding governments around the globe. He used that passion and began to look at how it affects people’s daily lives, specifically within public administration, public relations, and statistical analysis.After graduation, he founded GymCrew, LLC, an acrobatics company that seeks to elevate the public’s interest in the sport, the desire to train, and ensures accessibility of the sport for everyone, everywhere. Throughout his 7-year tenure with GymCrew, he has built a reputation for developing quality youth fitness programs with local government recreation centers, health care companies, and gymnastic clubs that educate on the importance of living a healthy life-style.From 2011-2014 Mubarak began serving on the AAU Gymnastics Executive Committee as the chair of freestyle acrobatics. He was responsible for the development, overall management, and strategic planning for the program. Mubarak also directed the live entertainment for the 2010, 2013, and 2014 Age Group National Championships Parade of Champions that featured performers from Walt Disney World, Universal Studios, SeaWorld, and Cirque Du Soleil. Throughout his term as Freestyle Chair, Mubarak consistently met or exceeded freestyle membership growth goals. In 2015, Mubarak began working at Orlando Metro Gymnastics as the boy’s team program director.
